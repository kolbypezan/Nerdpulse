General Questions

What is a NerdMiner?

A NerdMiner is a small, low-power device (usually based on an ESP32 microcontroller) designed for solo Bitcoin "lottery" mining. It's primarily an educational tool and a fun hobbyist gadget that lets you participate in the Bitcoin network and learn about Proof-of-Work. It displays mining stats on a small screen.

How much Bitcoin will I actually earn?

It's crucial to understand that earning significant Bitcoin with a NerdMiner is highly unlikely. Mining a Bitcoin block solo is like winning a major lottery due to the immense competition (network hashrate). View it as an educational tool and a fun hobby with a slim chance of a big win, not an income source. Please read our Mining Disclaimer.

Is it safe to leave my NerdMiner running 24/7?

Yes! NerdMiners are designed for continuous operation. They consume very little power (around 1-1.5W), similar to an LED night light, so they won't significantly impact your electricity bill.

What do I need to use a NerdMiner?

You'll need:

  • A NerdPulse Miner.
  • A USB power source (e.g., phone charger, computer USB port) and a USB-A cable (usually included).
  • A 2.4GHz Wi-Fi network with internet access.
  • A Bitcoin wallet address (to receive any potential block rewards).
  • A device (phone or computer) to perform the initial Wi-Fi setup.

Technical & Troubleshooting

My NerdMiner isn't connecting to Wi-Fi. What do I do?

Common solutions include:

  • Double-check your Wi-Fi password is correct during setup.
  • Ensure your Wi-Fi network is 2.4GHz (most NerdMiners don't support 5GHz).
  • Make sure the miner is within good range of your Wi-Fi router.
  • Try restarting the miner and your router.
  • Consult our Setup Guide or ask in our Community Discord.

What does the hashrate (e.g., 49KH/s) mean?

Hashrate is the speed at which your miner can perform hashing operations (guesses) per second. KH/s means KiloHashes per second (thousands of hashes). While 49KH/s is great for a tiny device, the Bitcoin network's total hashrate is in the ExaHashes per second (quintillions of hashes), which illustrates the lottery aspect.
